Snake Plant

Also known as mother-in-law's tongue, the Snake plant is admired for its upright, sword-like foliage with variegated shades of green.
This plant is best for home as it is incredibly resilient and perfect for the tropical and subtropical temperatures found throughout the country.


Spider Plant

Spider plants are popular for their long, arching leaves and are easy to grow plants in India. They’re well-suited for the climate because they thrive in warm temperatures and bright, indirect sunlight. They also don’t need constant watering and can bounce back even if you forget to water them sometimes. You can find many different varieties of spider plants.


Areca Palm

Areca palms or butterfly palms are quite common plants to grow indoors in India, and for a good reason. They do well in indirect light, need watering only when the top inch of the soil feels dry, and love moisture in the air. As long as you don’t overwater your plant, it’ll keep on thriving and spreading its beauty.


Baby Rubber Plant

Peperomias, or Baby rubber plants, are incredibly low maintenance and perfect for the Indian climate. They’re not actually trees but have the same shiny, waxy leaves and can tolerate low-light conditions. They don’t need constant watering, either.


String of Pearls

The String of Pearls is a lovely indoor plant that suits the Indian climate. It has wire-like stems covered in pearl-like rounded and fleshy leaves. This succulent is resilient and easy to care for. It can tolerate some drought, too, and does well in bright, indirect sunlight.
